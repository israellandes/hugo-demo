CompTIA A+

This article is Chapter 2 in the CompTIA A+ certification course on Alison.com that teaches Safety for You and Computer Components.

Common IT Tools

Working with hardware

  • Screwd rivers
  • Tweezers
  • Multimeters - ability to measure voltage, current and resistance.
  • Chip extractors - removing componets from motherboard
  • Storage canisters
  • Software tools

Electrostatic Discharge

The same static discharge you feel when you rub your feet on carpet and touch someone or something that releases the energry stored. This same shock can cause significant damage to computer systems.

• Danger to electronic components

  • To feel this shock the charge has to be approx. 3,000 V.

• Computer chips

  • A computuer chip can be damaged with as little as 200 V.

• Best practices

Handling Components

You can never really elimante ALL static build up, but you can minimize it.

• Humidifiers Placed in shops you work that place moister in air. The goal is to get humidity to around 50%. The dryer the air, the more static The wetter the air, the more opportunity for chrosion and rust.

• Antistatic bags

  • For carry computers/components between work stations.

• Carpet spray

  • Spray that can be used on carpet to reduce static electricity.

• ESD mats

  • Mats connect to grounding plug and work station as to help prevent static build up.

• ESD straps

  • Most popular, you wear a ESD start around your wrist which contains a resister that absorbs static electrcity. Next you use the ESD strap clamp and attach it to the outside metal on the computer casing. This provides constant grounding when servicing a computer, eliminating static build up.

• Equipment grounding

  • Always be on lookout for latest and greatest methods availible for grounding equipment.

• Self grounding

  • Connect grounding strap to the grounding plug of a power outlet.

Personal Saftey

• Personal protective equipment

  • Goggles
  • Gloves
  • ESD Strap
  • Air Filter Mask
  • First Aid Kit
  • Hearing Protection

• Chemicals

  • Any chemical you don’t know how to use, read up on so you can maintane saftey.
  • Look at the Material Saftey Data Sheet of each product
  • Compressed air
  • Cleaning solutions
  • Read lables, follow instructions
  • Leaky batteries and chrosion.

• Cables and trip hazards

  • You will encounter numerous different cord setups
  • Cable managment, bundle neatly
  • Never leave out or risk trip hazards

• Environmental risks

  • All components have an electrical source so be mindful of disposing and using.

• Electrical safety

  • Lock-out Tag-out procedures when working in groups.
  • YOU verify electricity is shut off before operating.
  • You always have a risk of fire when electricity is involved.
  • Adhere to the National Electrical Code (NEC) & CRF OSHA standards.
National Electrical Code CRF OSHA

• Fire safety

  • Keep fire BC-grade (Chemical & Electrical) fire estinquisher on hand.

• Jewelry removal

  • Metals conduct, so decrease your chances be removing metals.

• Lifting techniques

  • Use proper lifting tequniques, back straight and with legs.
  • Portible dollys or carts if needed.

High Voltage

Always be mindful of electrricty, and proper perpocsions shoudl be used when working with high voltage.

• Power supplies

  • Most common
  • Devices have capacitors which hold eletrical charge even when devices are unplugged.
  • They are used to convert 120 V of alternating current into 3.3 V, 5 V, or 12 V of direct voltage to operate equipment.

• CRT monitors

  • Most computor have a monitor
  • They require 50,000 to create images.
  • Don’t open unless you have qualified training
  • Never open a montior

• Warnings

  • Alwasy be aware of manufacture lables and warnings.
  • Never work on high voltage equip unless you’ve had specialized training
  • Be able to identify nonetheless

Erganomics

Reducing stressors and repettative strains for end users.

Preventing discomfort due to repetitive strain injuries • Eye care

  • Take frequent brakes
  • Eyes off monitor and focus on something else every 15 - 20 minutes.

• Posture

  • Sitting up straight
  • Feet flat on floor
  • Thighs and back at 90 degrees
  • Use an adjustable height desk.

• Keyboard and mouse positioning

  • Make sure keyboard not to high or low
  • Make sure everything is within arms reach

• Monitor position

  • Eye level

• Frequent breaks

  • Stretch
