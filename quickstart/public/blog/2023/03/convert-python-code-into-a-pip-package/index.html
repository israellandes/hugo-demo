What is the CompTIA A+ Certification?

2023-03-07 524 words 3 mins read

CompTIA A+

This article describes the CompTIA A+ certification.

What is the CompTIA A+ certification?

According to https://www.comptia.org/, “CompTIA A+ is the industry standard for establishing a career in IT”.

The whole point of the certification is to teach new IT trainees how to be problem solvers so they can tackle issues relating to core technologies from security to networking to virtualization and more.

CompTIA A+ is considered the only IT industry recognized credential with “performance testing” that proves the professionals can think on their feet when performing critical IT support tasks.

Such standard is trusted by employers globally when it comes to finding the go-to person in technology management & technical support roles. CompTIA A+ is the most common IT credential that appears job listings compared to any other IT credential.

How do I obtain a certification?

The CompTIA A+ Core Series cetification requires candidates to pass both exams: Core 1 (220-1101) and Core 2 (220-1102), the following content emphasizes these technologies and skills IT pros need in order to support hybrid workforces.

  • Reliance on SaaS applications for remote work
  • Large ammounts of training on troubleshooting and how to remotely detect and correct common hardware, software, or connection problems
  • Complex core technologies such as IoT device security, data management, cloud virtualization and scripting
  • Multiple operating systems ae used by companies and technicians on a regular basis, which includes the major systems, their use cases, and how to keep them running correctly.
  • Shows the always changing nature of the job role, where many dutys are issued to specialized providers as certified a IT profressional needs to be able to assess whether it’s best to fix something on site, or would it be more cost and time effective to send proprietary technologies directly to vendors

What skills will I obtain and master?

The 9 main skills that you will master and validate with CompTIA A+ include the following:

HARDWARE

  • Troubleshooting, using, and attaching hardware components and devices, including learning knowledge about different devices is needed to support remote workforce

OPERATING SYSTEMS

Installing and supporting Windows OS which includes client support and command line. Configuring systems, imaging and troubleshooting for Mac OS, Linux OS, Chrome OS, and Android.

SOFTWARE TROUBLESHOOTING

Troubleshooting PC and mobile device problems including malware, common OS, and security issues.

NETWORKING

Describe different types of networks and connections such as TCP/IP, WIFI and SOHO

TROUBLESHOOTING

Fixing real-world network and device issues efficiently and quickly

SECURITY

Finding and protecting against security vulnerabilities for network connections and their devices

MOBILE DEVICES

Install & setting up laptops and other mobile devices and support various applications while ensureing connectivity for end-users

VIRTUALIZATION & CLOUD COMPUTING

Comparing & contrasting cloud computing concepts, while seting up virtualization on the client-side

OPERATIONAL PROCEDURES

Applying best practices for safety, communication, professionalism and environmental impacts.

How much does the exams cost?

At the time of writing, the pricing is around $500 total.

How long does the certification last?

Renewall is required every 3 years.
